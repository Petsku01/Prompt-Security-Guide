## Academic Research Papers

### Defense Against Prompt Injection Attack by Leveraging Attack Techniques (ArXiv 2411.00459)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.00459  
**Authors:** Yulin Chen et al.  
**Summary:** Novel defense approach that inverts prompt injection attack techniques for defensive purposes. The paper demonstrates that attack and defense methods share similar design goals - both aim to make models ignore unwanted instructions and execute wanted ones. By repeating the attack process with original instructions rather than injected ones, they achieve state-of-the-art training-free defense results.  
**Relevance:** Innovative defensive technique that could be implemented in our guidance framework. Shows how understanding attack mechanisms can directly inform defense strategies.

### International AI Safety Report 2026 (ArXiv 2602.21012)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21012  
**Lead Author:** Yoshua Bengio (Turing Award winner)  
**Summary:** Comprehensive international collaboration (30+ countries) synthesizing current scientific evidence on AI capabilities, emerging risks, and safety. Follow-up to the 2025 report that was the largest global collaboration on AI safety to date.  
**Relevance:** Authoritative source for latest AI safety research and risk assessments. Essential for understanding the broader security context beyond just prompt injection.
