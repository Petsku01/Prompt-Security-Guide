## Key Themes Observed

1. **Defense-in-depth is consensus**: No single defense is sufficient. Layered mitigations (input validation + output filtering + architectural constraints) are the prevailing approach.
2. **Architectural defenses gaining traction**: Design patterns, JATMO (task-specific models), and separation-of-concerns approaches go beyond prompt-level mitigations.
3. **Over-defense is a real problem**: Models that reject too aggressively (false positives on benign inputs) are a practical deployment concern. PIGuard addresses this directly.
4. **Proactive testing emerging**: Prompt fuzzing and integrated attack/defense benchmarks (TeleAI-Safety) are shifting the field from reactive to proactive defense evaluation.
5. **Agentic security is the new frontier**: As LLMs become agents with tool access, injection vectors propagate beyond single prompts into multi-step workflows and protocol exploits.
6. **Fundamental limits acknowledged**: Gödel-inspired arguments suggest perfect defense is impossible — framing the goal as risk reduction, not elimination.
