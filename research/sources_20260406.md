# Prompt Security Research Sources — 2026-04-06

## 1) Defend against indirect prompt injection attacks (Microsoft Learn)
- **URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/security/zero-trust/sfi/defend-indirect-prompt-injection
- **Brief summary:** Microsoft’s Zero Trust guidance focused on indirect prompt injection in enterprise copilots and agentic systems. Recommends a defense-in-depth stack: prompt shields, spotlighting/data marking, plan-drift detection, critic agents, tool-chain analysis, policy guardrails, information-flow control (IFC), least privilege, short-lived privileges, and human-in-the-loop checks.
- **Why it’s relevant:** Practical, implementation-oriented blueprint for production systems dealing with untrusted external content and tool-enabled agents.

## 2) LLM Prompt Injection Prevention Cheat Sheet (OWASP)
- **URL:** https://cheatsheetseries.owasp.org/cheatsheets/LLM_Prompt_Injection_Prevention_Cheat_Sheet.html
- **Brief summary:** Consolidated taxonomy of prompt injection patterns (direct/indirect, obfuscation, multi-turn, agent/tool, multimodal, RAG poisoning) and layered mitigations (input/output controls, structured prompts, least privilege, monitoring, HITL). Includes concrete engineering examples and deployment checklists.
- **Why it’s relevant:** High-signal practitioner reference that maps threat classes to controls, useful as a baseline hardening checklist.

## 3) International AI Safety Report 2026 (arXiv)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21012v1
- **Brief summary:** Multi-author international synthesis of evidence on general-purpose AI capabilities, emerging risks, and safety approaches, produced under AI Safety Summit mandate with broad expert representation.
- **Why it’s relevant:** Authoritative macro-level context for risk governance and safety priorities, useful for positioning prompt security within broader AI safety and policy work.

## 4) International AI Safety Report (2025 edition) (arXiv)
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.17805
- **Brief summary:** First edition of the international synthesis report covering evidence on advanced AI capabilities, risks, and safety considerations.
- **Why it’s relevant:** Baseline reference for trend comparison between 2025 and 2026 framing of safety risks and mitigations.

## 5) Many-shot jailbreaking (Anthropic Research)
- **URL:** https://www.anthropic.com/research/many-shot-jailbreaking
- **Brief summary:** Research note on long-context jailbreak dynamics showing attack effectiveness increases with many in-context demonstrations; discusses mitigation attempts and limits of simple fine-tuning-only defenses.
- **Why it’s relevant:** Important empirical insight into scaling-related failure modes and why prompt-layer and preprocessing defenses are needed alongside model-level safeguards.

## 6) AI Risk Management Framework (NIST) + GenAI Profile reference
- **URL:** https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework
- **Brief summary:** NIST AI RMF overview and implementation resources, including mention of the Generative AI Profile (NIST-AI-600-1) for genAI-specific risks.
- **Why it’s relevant:** Widely adopted risk management structure for integrating technical prompt-security controls into governance, lifecycle, and assurance processes.

## 7) Multilayer Framework for Good Cybersecurity Practices for AI (ENISA)
- **URL:** https://www.enisa.europa.eu/publications/multilayer-framework-for-good-cybersecurity-practices-for-ai
- **Brief summary:** ENISA framework proposing layered cybersecurity practices for AI: foundational cyber controls, AI-specific cyber controls, and sector-specific overlays.
- **Why it’s relevant:** EU-oriented strategic framework that helps align prompt-security controls with broader operational cybersecurity and compliance expectations.
