# Prompt Security Research Sources — 2026-04-08

## 1) International AI Safety Report 2026
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.21012v1
- **Brief summary:** A large multi-stakeholder synthesis report (29 nations + international organizations, 100+ experts) summarizing current evidence on general-purpose AI capabilities, emerging risks, and safety approaches.
- **Why it’s relevant:** Provides high-level, policy-to-technical framing for AI risk and safety work in 2026, useful as a foundational reference for prompt-security governance context.

## 2) LLM Prompt Injection Prevention Cheat Sheet (OWASP)
- **URL:** https://cheatsheetseries.owasp.org/cheatsheets/LLM_Prompt_Injection_Prevention_Cheat_Sheet.html
- **Brief summary:** Practical guidance on prompt injection threat models and layered mitigations (input validation, structured prompts, output checks, HITL controls, least privilege, monitoring), with implementation examples.
- **Why it’s relevant:** One of the most actionable practitioner resources for prompt injection defenses in production LLM systems.

## 3) Defend against indirect prompt injection attacks (Microsoft Learn)
- **URL:** https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/security/zero-trust/sfi/defend-indirect-prompt-injection
- **Brief summary:** Enterprise defense-in-depth pattern focused on indirect injection from untrusted external content, including prompt shields, content isolation, plan-drift detection, critic agents, tool-chain analysis, and least-privilege controls.
- **Why it’s relevant:** Strong operational guidance for real-world copilots/agents where external content and tool use create practical injection risk.

## 4) Cisco LLM Security Leaderboard announcement
- **URL:** https://blogs.cisco.com/ai/llm-security-leaderboard
- **Brief summary:** Introduces a public benchmark-style leaderboard focused on model resistance to single-turn and multi-turn adversarial attacks, with taxonomy-based breakdowns and transparent methodology.
- **Why it’s relevant:** Useful for comparative model-security assessment and for selecting model baselines before adding application-level guardrails.

## 5) International AI Safety Report 2025: Second Key Update
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.19863
- **Brief summary:** Tracks 2025 progress in technical safeguards and risk management, including stronger safeguards in frontier model releases, advances in adversarial training/data curation/monitoring, and growth in institutional safety frameworks.
- **Why it’s relevant:** Gives near-term historical trend data that contextualizes 2026 developments and helps identify which safety controls are maturing.
